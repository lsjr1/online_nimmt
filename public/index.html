How to Play

Objective: Finish the game with the fewest penalty points (bullheads). The game lasts for 10 rounds.

The Setup

Four cards are placed face-up in the center to start four distinct rows. Each player receives a hand of 7 cards.

Playing a Turn

  • Simultaneous Play: Every player secretly selects one card from their hand to play.
  • The Reveal: Once all players have locked in their choice, all cards are revealed at the same time.
  • Resolution Order: The revealed cards are processed in ascending order, from lowest value to highest value.

Placement Rules

When it is your card's turn to be placed onto the board, it must follow these strict rules:

  1. Ascending Order: Your card must be higher in value than the last card in the row you are placing it in.
  2. Smallest Difference: If your card can legally fit in multiple rows, it must go into the row where the difference between your card and the row's last card is the smallest.

The Penalties

  • The 6th Card Rule: A row can only hold 5 cards safely. If your card becomes the 6th card in a row, you must take the first 5 cards as penalty points. Your played card then becomes the new first card of that row.
  • The "Too Low" Rule: If you play a card that is lower than the end cards of all four rows, you must take a penalty. The game automatically assigns you the row with the fewest penalty points currently on the board. Your card replaces that row.
